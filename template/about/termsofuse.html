The risks associated with using our services

Risk #1: Capital Loss

Businesses, at any stage, can fail, and if that happens, you will lose the entirety of the investment you made. You should never invest more money than you can afford to lose without having to change your standard of living.

Risk #2: Loss of Account Access

You may forget your account credentials or someone may steal your login information and carry out unauthorized transactions.

Risk #3: Other Cybersecurity Risks, Including Malicious Activities

Online platforms and third-party service providers may be vulnerable to hacking or other malicious activities. For instance, in August 2016, nearly 120,000 units representing $72 million worth of bitcoins were stolen from the Hong Kong-based exchange Bitfinex, leading to an immediate 23% price drop. A year earlier, in September 2015, BitPay lost around $1.8 million worth of bitcoins due to a phishing attack.

Risk #4: Malfunction of Critical Internet Infrastructure

The threat landscape is increasingly characterized by organized groups or non-state actors and individuals resorting to asymmetric attacks made possible by the universal connectivity offered by the Internet and the availability of tools and attack information. Loss of control over technology resulting from globalization, the need for online accessibility, and foreign ownership of critical infrastructures also increase vulnerabilities.

Risk #5: No Guarantee of Outcome

We analyze average performance based on past overall results. Individual results may vary. It should be remembered that past performance does not guarantee future results.

Risk #6: Legislative Risks

Legislative risk is the possibility that government regulations or laws may significantly alter the business outlook of one or more companies. These changes can have a negative impact on investments in that company. Legislative risk may result directly from government action or changes in the demand structure of the company's members.

Risk #7: Economic Recession Risk

A recession is a macroeconomic term referring to a significant downturn in overall economic activity within a given region. It is typically recognized as two consecutive quarters of economic decline, as reflected by GDP along with monthly indicators such as rising unemployment.

Risk #8: Unknown (Other Unidentified Risks)

These types of risks pose potentially greater risk simply because they cannot be anticipated based on past experiences or surveys. Unknowns are phenomena that cannot be expected because there has been no prior experience or theoretical basis to anticipate these phenomena.

We take steps to mitigate all possible risks to make our business model as adaptable as possible.
However, it's important to understand that there is no risk-free environment in business.
